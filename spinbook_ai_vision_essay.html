Vision statement — Spinbook

The music was always the easy part

My co-founder has been a DJ for years. I've watched her build entire nights from scratch, curating the right set, reading the room, holding the energy of a crowd in her hands. What I didn't understand, until I sat with her and really looked, was everything that happened before the music started.

The texts. The back-and-forth negotiations conducted over Instagram DMs. The contracts that were either PDFs attached to emailsor nothing at all. The deposits paid through Venmo, the final payments handed in cash in a backroom at 2am. The no-shows she'd witnessed. The gigs that fell apart at the last minute because a venue's word was the only paper trail that existed.

The creative work behind nightlife is invisible. The infrastructure that should protect it simply doesn't exist.

I believe AI is most powerful not when it replaces what humans do well, but when it handles what they were never meant to do in the first place. Artists aren't supposed to be contract lawyers. DJs aren't supposed to be accountants. Venue managers shouldn't be chasing payments across three different apps while simultaneously running a 300-person event.

The signs of this future are already around us. I see it when a first-generation small business owner uses AI to draft a legal agreement they could never have afforded before. I see it when a freelance creative can navigate a tax question without paying $300 for an hour with a professional. I see it when the asymmetry between people who have access to expertise and people who don't begins, even slightly, to close.

That's what Spinbook is, at its core. Not a booking app. Not another SaaS dashboard. It's the infrastructure layer that gives nightlife operators, the people who build culture out of nothing, who turn empty warehouses into memories, the same operational foundation that enterprise companies have always taken for granted. Booking requests, e-contracts, deposits, payouts: handled. Automatically. Correctly. With a paper trail.

My hope for AI is simple: that the next great DJ doesn't lose a gig over a missing contract. That the next promoter doesn't lose their margin to a payment dispute. That the unglamorous work of running a creative business stops being a tax on creativity.

The best nights I've ever experienced started with someone who cared deeply about what they were making and had the space to focus on making it. AI, done right, gives people that space. It takes the operational weight off the people who carry culture and lets them put it back where it belongs: into the work.

That's the future we're building toward. One booking, one contract, one protected artist at a time.

Spinbook — submitted for the ChatGPT Fund, April 2026
